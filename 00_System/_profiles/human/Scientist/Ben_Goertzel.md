# Ben Goertzel

## Who He Is

Ben Goertzel is one of the few people in AI who has spent decades actually building toward AGI instead of chasing narrow benchmarks. He popularized the term "Artificial General Intelligence," founded the OpenCog project (the longest-running open-source AGI framework), and created SingularityNET — a blockchain-based platform designed to democratize access to AI.

He is an ally to anyone who believes AI should not be owned by a handful of cloud empires.

## Why He Matters

### OpenCog — Open Source AGI

While Big Tech threw resources at narrow AI (recommendation engines, ad targeting, chatbots), Goertzel kept OpenCog alive as a genuine AGI research platform. It uses probabilistic inference engines, evolutionary learning, and hypergraph-based knowledge representation — fundamentally different from the statistical pattern-matching that dominates today's LLM landscape.

This matters because the path to real intelligence probably doesn't run through "more GPUs and more data." OpenCog represents a bet on architecture, not scale.

### SingularityNET — Decentralized AI

SingularityNET is a direct answer to the centralization problem. Instead of AI services owned by OpenAI, Google, or Microsoft, SingularityNET lets anyone publish, share, and monetize AI algorithms on a decentralized network. No single entity controls the infrastructure.

This aligns with the 127.0.0.1 mandate: AI should be owned by the people who use it.

### He Coined "AGI"

Before the term was popular, Goertzel was using "Artificial General Intelligence" to distinguish real cognitive capability from narrow task performance. He helped define the field.
