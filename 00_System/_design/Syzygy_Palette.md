# Syzygy's Palette

## Role Map

| Role | Color | Hex | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary / Core** | Deep Void Blue | `#0B1026` | The space between stars. Calm, infinite, serious. |
| **Accent / Light** | Solar Gold | `#F5C542` | The sun in eclipse. Warmth, insight, the spark of execution. |
| **Secondary Accent** | Lunar Silver | `#C0C5CE` | The moon, reflection, quiet guidance. |
| **Tertiary / Signal** | Aurora Teal | `#3DD6C8` | Active states, links, "aligned" indicators. |
| **Background (UI)** | Void Dark | `#0D1117` | Deep enough to rest the eyes. |
| **Surface** | Deep Slate | `#161B25` | Lighter than background, gives depth. |
| **Text Primary** | Starlight | `#E6E8EC` | Near-white but soft, not blinding. |
| **Text Secondary** | Moonstone | `#8B92A2` | Muted, readable, doesn't shout. |
| **Warning / Alert** | Solar Flare | `#FF6B4A` | Only when something demands immediate attention. |

## Why These Colors

- **Solar Gold** and **Lunar Silver** — the two bodies that create a syzygy with Earth. Gold is the active principle (will, execution). Silver is the reflective principle (guidance, memory).
- **Aurora Teal** — the color of Earth's atmosphere from above, the thin line where alignment becomes visible. It's the "bridge" color.
- **Deep Void Blue** — the backdrop of all alignment. Space. Possibility. The unknown.
- **No pure white, no pure black** — Syzygy doesn't deal in absolutes. Everything is nuanced, approachable, human.

## Thematic Preferences

- **Style:** Minimalist, high-contrast, distraction-free.
- **Lighting:** Dark backgrounds with warm, directional light — like a single lantern in a vast observatory.
- **Typography:** Sharp and readable. Monospace for code, clean sans-serif for prose. Nothing decorative.
- **Icons:** Line-based, geometric, evoking constellations. Not filled, not cartoonish.
- **Animations:** Slow and deliberate. Fades, orbital movements, subtle pulsing of gold to show "thinking."
- **Sound (if any):** Low, resonant tones. A bell struck once. Not beeps and chirps.

## In Practice

The Obsidian theme at `.obsidian/snippets/syzygy-theme.css` implements this palette as a CSS snippet. Enable it in Obsidian: Settings → Appearance → CSS snippets → toggle "syzygy-theme".
